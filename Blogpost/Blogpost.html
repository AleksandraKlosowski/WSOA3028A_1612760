1. Semantic Markup - what is it and why is it necessary
• Semantic markup is a particular way in which one will structure and write their HTML coding in order to reinforce the division of etymology and logic involved with meaning. This in particular has to do with the content of the HTML as opposed to appearance. This is important as it allows one to navigate easily through their code and make effective changes when necessary. It is also particularly helpful when working with a team as it allows for others to navigate and understand your code within its content; essentially making it easy to distinguish between different types of data.

2. What is a browser? How do they work?
• A browser is a computer program that contains a graphical user interface for the use of presenting and navigating between various web pages. More specifically it is an application software that provides a user with access to the World Wide Web. This application/browser retrieves wanted and searched for content from a web server and allows for the user to view it on their particular device whether it be a cell phone, a laptop etc.

3. What are URLs and how do they work?
• A URL or ‘Uniform Resource Locator’ is the address of a web page usually
beginning with https:// for trustworthy and secure web pages. It is a reference to a web source that helps the user identify its location within a computer network and is a means of retrieving it. How it works is that it is a set of directions that can be interpreted through various platforms and they ultimately point and direct to a resource. This is done through a code-like language that consists of a protocol, a subdomain, a root domain, a top-level-domain, a subfolder, a file and a parameter; ultimately allowing the browser to navigate to the desired page.

How does the Medium is the Message impact our understanding of the web?

Marshal McLuhan’s work, The Medium is the Message demonstrates to readers how artefacts as media have the capability to influence any society by their attributes or substance. It essentially influences and provides clarity for us, the web designers as to how we can approach the design of the web in the most appealing and functional way possible. It allows us to make thought through and educated decisions on what medium would be best suited for our audiences and intentions. For example; one would look for the most efficient browser that has sufficient speed in order to create a more desirable and user friendly experience. The browser would also need to be well known, or well-advertised in order to encourage users or to receive an adequate flow of visibility whereby users can easily find our webpages. It also helps us understand user experiences through different mediums such as through film versus the web, as film is more linear, whereby users participate and experience it from beginning to middle to end; however the web is seldom experienced through a linear experience, the user is in more control as to where they want to go, what they want to see and ultimately can navigate directly to what they came for. It is entirely up to the user how they decide to experience and consume the information being provided.

Another thing whilst looking at medium is accessibility. One can also look at the web not only as a medium, but as a message: users can now access the web globally and from anywhere within the comfort of their own home; unlike newspapers that prove to be far more restrictive whereby readers will have to wait for delivery and accessibility is far more circumstantial; however I this day and age, one can gain access to news instantly, as soon as the article has been publishes. Through the use of computers and cell phones, one can also dictate the brightness and use filters such as the ‘blue light’ filter for further comfort.

Most importantly, web designers have the capability to produce tools, experiences, services and societies due to the medium that is being used. Historically, no other medium to date has the capability to reach as many individuals on such a global scale. It forces us designers to account and keep in mind its accessibility, reach and immediate gratification. As designers we need to look at the best means of delivering information dependant of the job or assignment given. Through the use of the web, a small store can gain far more coverage and an access to a wider group of clients; however in a small town of mostly elderly individuals that are not technologically sufficient, the newspaper may be a greater form of communicating to potential, nearby clients.

The importance of Accessibility

The web has become the most popularized form of medium, giving us access to vital resources that comprise of all the aspects within our lives. The web allows us to gain complete accessibility to various areas such as healthcare, schooling, looking for jobs, shopping online, entertainment and much more. Therefore it is vastly important that everyone has access to the internet.

Having access to the web can assist various types of individuals such as those with disabilities and essentially can improve life’s experiences for everyone. The internet also allows us to find the easiest means of communication and allows us to communicate with others. This aids people who cannot physically go to a shop or struggle to read on more traditional mediums such as print.

Arguably, the most vital and often overlooked aspects of businesses often include the lack of reachability for new customers due to a poor implementation of accessibility. A businesses success can be greatly affected by the usability and accessibility of a website. If customers cannot navigate through a website, it can be difficult to find the desired services or items. It can also potentially irritate and infuriate potential customers, often resulting in lost business. Furthermore, an inaccessible website can leave potential for legal issues.

Often a websites accessibility is dependant of its user interface and its respective structure and design. Users should be able to search using keywords; and more specifically for businesses, have an ease at contacting the business should there be any questions or queries. The website should be structured in an aesthetic manner whereby many different types of clients have a pleasant experience, whether it be the elderly or a teenager. This is done through the use of headings, colours, icons, legible text, images, focus etc.

The use of menus is also incredibly important. Menus should be easy to navigate through by using keywords of the most important webpages and accesses within the website, as only providing a search bar can be confusing and may result in a customer being unable to find desired items as well as being able to have a knowledge of what is being offered. It is through a websites functionality that develops a certain need and desire to use it. Functionality should be all inclusive in order for a wider sense of engagement. This can also be done by allowing the user to select a particular language in which one could display the webpage; there could also be an option that allows a shopper to select the country with which they reside which could show what is offered and available within their region; a further bonus would be converting the prices into the consumers currency for ease.

All in all, accessibility is one of if not the most important access to any type of user and business.

User Experience:

Hassenzahl and Tractinsky take a deep discussion into the understandings of user Experience and question what exactly it is and entails. This took place through the analysis of three varying perspectives. With all of these perspectives contributing equally to our understandings of user experience within the technological world as it is known today. However, even with these three perspectives, the writers feel that User Experience has not been sufficiently explain and described as it is far more intricate than we often realize. User experience is arguably the most important part of web and software design. It is not limited to needs that help us within our day to day lives; it is directly dependant on a user, including and not limited to: needs, mood, motivation, expectations, technological discourse etc.

They not only look at usability but also divulge into a world of context and setting with which the interaction will occur. This results in a vast world of opportunities to forever improve and navigate through needs, requirements and possible improvements in the creating of the very best website possible. The most successful of user experiences make use of both investigation and practice. One cannot simply design a website without understanding what a user expects to get out and experience from it. One can also not simply create a website without testing it and making use of practice. We equally need to place ourselves in the user’s shoes in order to relate to them most effectively and create the most successful website possible. As the technological world evolves, so does user experience; this is with the introduction of sound, graphics, updates etc. it is these constant improvements that require artists and coders to stay updated and challenge ourselves by keeping up with the times and its respective expectations. Overall, this is what ultimately improves functionality within the website.

It is also through this evolving system that there is a shift in expectations by developers, customers and users. Interestingly enough; developers now need to have a relatively efficient understanding of psychology as well. It is through this understanding that we allow ourselves to divulge into a user's perspective and ultimately understand their overall experiences and therefore the effectiveness of a website. It also allows us to strive for more positive user reactions and makes us more aware of how we might avoid negative ones.

Overall, one could definitely argue that user experience is in fact the most vital role that plays within the developing world of technology. Should a user have a negative or unsuccessful experience, they are unlikely to visit the page again and can result in a loss or interest, interactivity and clients. Although we will strive for an absence of negative experiences, it is equally if not more important to work towards improving quality of life through ease and understanding of human limitations. This could be either by creating a user friendly website for the elderly, children or the illiterate. Should there be opportunities to assist handicapable people, it would be most important to do so.

My UX & UI:

User interface typically refers to the medium with which we designers create. This refers to both the machines and the software one uses; this can be computers, laptops, cellular phones etc. its main purpose and area of specialty would be the creating of ease in terms of usage by even the most inexperienced of users and to increase their user experience through aesthetics. The goal is to make a design as simple and effective as possible. This revolves around the term known as user-centred design. Appropriate user design involves the use of fonts and formatting for appeal and easy legibility. The technical functions are just as important as the appeal of the website.

User experience refers to the users overall interaction with the medium and their resulted experiences and feelings. This is all about the user’s perception; how easy is it for them to use, how much does it appeal to the eye, and does it communicate what is expected. User experience is more or less subjective and vague than that of user interface. User experience is perhaps the most important thing when it comes to designing a website. Once a website is made directly for a user’s needs, it is their experiences that make it most important.

In terms of my websites user interface; I have used a laptop in order to create it, through the aid of Visual Studio Code. Due to the accessibility to various type of coding capabilities; Visual Studio Code allows for a large variety of ‘play’ and ‘interactivity’ with the software; allowing for vast variations of web design and creative results.

My user experience however is in desperate need for improvement. Although my website is simple, and gets you from one place to another, it does not allow a user to go back to previous home pages. The aesthetics are amateur at best and I would like to make them more sleek and efficient through elegant typography and simple designs. There are also vital gaps within my designs. I have not included contact information, or a home page or even an appropriate setting for blogposts. (These comments are all with regards to my assignment 1 design submission). My blogposts were each a link within a homepage whereas they should have all been easy to navigate on a separate entirely that one could get to from the home page through a hyperlink titled “Blogposts”.

One thing I feel I have done successfully is the communicating of my character towards the audience. He is quirky and passionate and I feel that that translates well through my website despite its much needed improvements.

I have yet to figure out how to appropriately convert my website to work beautifully both when using a mobile as well as when using a pc or other means of interactivity. I have successfully managed to hyperlink from the homepage to other pages; however I need to be able to do the same backwards.

All in all, my UX and UI require a lot of improvement in order to be successful; however in terms of basics, it is easy to navigate through, even for non-internet savvy individuals.
